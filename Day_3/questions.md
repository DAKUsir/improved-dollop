# Questions for day 3

1) What is NLP?
    - NLP stands for Natural Language Processing. It is a field of artificial intelligence that focuses on the interaction between computers and humans through natural language. The goal of NLP is to enable computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language in a valuable way. This involves tasks such as language translation, sentiment analysis, and chatbots, among others.
2) What is tokenization?
    - Tokenization is the process of breaking down text into smaller units called tokens. These tokens can be words, subwords, or characters, depending on the level of tokenization. Tokenization is a crucial step in NLP as it allows models to process and understand text data effectively. It helps in converting raw text into a format that can be easily analyzed and used for various NLP tasks.
3) What is vocabulary in tokenization?
    - Vocabulary in tokenization refers to the set of unique tokens that a model recognizes and uses for processing text. It is essentially a mapping of tokens to their corresponding indices in the model's embedding layer. The size of the vocabulary can impact the performance of the model, as a larger vocabulary may capture more nuances of language but can also lead to increased computational complexity.
4) What is GPT?
    - GPT stands for Generative Pre-trained Transformer. It is a type of language model developed by OpenAI that uses a transformer architecture to generate human-like text. GPT is pre-trained on a large corpus of text data and can be fine-tuned for various NLP tasks such as text generation, translation, and summarization. The model is designed to predict the next word in a sequence, making it effective for generating coherent and contextually relevant text.
5) What is BERT?
    - BERT stands for Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers. It is a language model developed by Google that uses a transformer architecture to understand the context of words in a sentence. Unlike GPT, which is unidirectional, BERT is bidirectional, meaning it considers both the left and right context of a word when processing text. This allows BERT to capture more nuanced meanings and relationships between words, making it effective for tasks such as question answering and sentiment analysis.
6) What is the difference between GPT and BERT? 
    - The main difference between GPT and BERT lies in their architecture and training objectives. GPT is a unidirectional model that generates text by predicting the next word in a sequence, while BERT is a bidirectional model that understands the context of words by considering both the left and right context. GPT is primarily used for text generation tasks, whereas BERT is more suited for understanding and analyzing text, making it effective for tasks like question answering and sentiment analysis.
7) What is the importance of tokenization in NLP?
    - Tokenization is crucial in NLP because it allows models to process and understand text data effectively. It breaks down raw text into smaller units (tokens) that can be analyzed and used for various NLP tasks. Proper tokenization can help capture the meaning and context of words, which is essential for tasks like language translation, sentiment analysis, and chatbots. Additionally, tokenization can help reduce the complexity of language by breaking down words into subwords or characters, allowing models to handle out-of-vocabulary words and improve performance.
8) Where do we import AutoTokenizer from and what is its purpose?
    - AutoTokenizer is imported from the Hugging Face Transformers library. Its purpose is to provide a convenient way to load pre-trained tokenizers for various language models. AutoTokenizer automatically selects the appropriate tokenizer based on the model you specify, allowing you to easily tokenize text data for use with different models without needing to manually configure the tokenizer for each one.
9) What is Subword tokenization and how does it differ from word-level tokenization?
    - Subword tokenization is a method of breaking down words into smaller units called subwords. This approach allows models to handle out-of-vocabulary words by breaking them down into known subword units. In contrast, word-level tokenization treats each word as a separate token, which can lead to issues when encountering words that are not in the model's vocabulary. Subword tokenization helps improve the model's ability to understand and generate text by capturing more nuanced meanings and relationships between words, while word-level tokenization may struggle with rare or unseen words.
10) What is the purpose of the tokenizer in NLP?
    - The purpose of the tokenizer in NLP is to convert raw text into a format that can be processed by machine learning models. The tokenizer breaks down text into smaller units (tokens) and maps them to their corresponding indices in the model's vocabulary. This allows the model to understand and analyze the text data effectively. The tokenizer also handles tasks such as padding and truncation to ensure that input sequences are of the appropriate length for the model. Overall, the tokenizer is a crucial component in the NLP pipeline that enables models to work with text data efficiently.
